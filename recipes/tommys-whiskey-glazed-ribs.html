Tommy's Whiskey-Glazed Ribs

"Savor the Bold: Tommy's Whiskey-Glazed Ribs at The Garrison Tavern."

Indulge in a dish that embodies the unyielding spirit of Tommy Shelby himself. Our succulent ribs, slow-cooked to perfection and basted in a rich whiskey glaze, are a testament to Birmingham's finest. Each bite is a journey through flavor, smokiness, and a touch of rebellion—a true Peaky Blinders delight.

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of pork baby back ribs
  • 1 cup whiskey (preferably a quality bourbon)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ribs:
    • Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs for tenderness.
    • Season both sides of the ribs with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and chili powder.
  2. Slow Cook the Ribs:
    • Preheat your grill to low heat (around 250°F/120°C).
    • Place the ribs on the grill, bone side down.
    • Cover and cook for 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the meat is tender and begins to pull away from the bones.
  3. Prepare the Whiskey Glaze:
    • In a saucepan, combine whiskey, brown sugar, tomato paste, apple cider vinegar, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
    • Simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens into a glaze (about 15-20 minutes).
    • Remove from heat.
  4. Glaze the Ribs:
    • During the last 30 minutes of grilling, brush the whiskey glaze generously over the ribs.
    • Continue grilling, turning and basting every 10 minutes, until the ribs are glossy and the glaze is caramelized.
  5. Serve:
    • Remove the ribs from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes.
    • Cut between the bones and serve hot with extra whiskey glaze on the side.
